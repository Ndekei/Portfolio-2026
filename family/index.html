III

The Stones We Stand On

Our Values

Nine convictions we return to — every day, every week, every year, every generation.

  1. 01

    God First

    Every decision, large or small, begins and ends at His feet.

    "You shall have no other gods before Me." — Exodus 20:3

  2. 02

    Love

    The language of our home — spoken freely, modeled daily, never withheld.

    "Above all, love each other deeply." — 1 Peter 4:8

  3. 03

    Honesty

    With ourselves, with each other, with the world — no version we hide.

    "The Lord delights in people who are trustworthy." — Proverbs 12:22

  4. 04

    Truthfulness

    The truth, spoken in love, is the only foundation worth building on.

    "Speak the truth in love." — Ephesians 4:15

  5. 05

    Transparency

    Nothing lived in the dark. We walk in the light, and so shall our children.

    "Walk in the light, as He is in the light." — 1 John 1:7

  6. 06

    Continuous Growth

    We are becoming. Each day, better — in mind, in spirit, in craft.

    "Grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord." — 2 Peter 3:18

  7. 07

    Wisdom

    Knowledge is cheap; wisdom is earned. We pursue it like treasure.

    "The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom." — Proverbs 9:10

  8. 08

    Patience

    We build slowly, deliberately, and with care. Great things take time.

    "Love is patient, love is kind." — 1 Corinthians 13:4

  9. 09

    Unity

    One household, one heart, one direction. We move together or not at all.

    "How good and pleasant it is when God's people live in unity." — Psalm 133:1

IV

Raised, Not Merely Grown

The Children We Will Raise

Before they arrive, we are already building the home they will call theirs — stone by stone, prayer by prayer.

i.

Raised in the Ways of God

Faith will not be an inheritance of habit but a living thing — taught at the table, practiced in the everyday, anchored in the Word. They will know Christ before they know the world.

ii.

Rooted in Identity

They will know who they are and where they come from. They will carry the name Ndekei not as a weight but as a blessing — a story written before them, to be carried further by them.

iii.

Disciplined in Mind, Body & Spirit

Educated deeply and widely. Strong in body and stewardship. Unafraid of hard work, hard questions, and hard truths. We are raising people of substance — not just performance.

iv.

Formed to Love & to Lead

We will model love before we demand it, and lead by serving. Our children will leave this home able to build homes of their own — full of faith, craft, and quiet dignity.

VI

A Covenant, Not a Souvenir

To Our Children

Our dear children,

Before you knew the world, we knew of you. Before you bore our name, we were already building a home for you — stone by stone, prayer by prayer, dream by dream.

This page is not a keepsake. It is a covenant. We write it now so that, years from today, when the world asks who you are, you will have your answer ready.

You are a Ndekei. You are loved. You are known. You are set apart — not by what you own, but by Whom you serve.

We will raise you in the way of the Lord — not because it is tradition, but because it is true. We will teach you to love as we have been loved, to work with craft, to speak with honesty, and to carry yourself with the quiet dignity of those who know where they come from.

When you fall, come home. When you rise, come home. This house will always be a place where God is at the center, where love is the language we speak, and where your name will always mean something.

We are praying for you already.

With everything we are,

Your mother and father
Lisa & Victor Ndekei
Nairobi · 2026
